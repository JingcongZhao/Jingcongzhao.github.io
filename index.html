My name is Jingcong and this is where I write my thoughts on start-ups and marketing. I am really interested in harnessing the power of technology to solve persistent social problems, i.e. unemployment, poverty, lack of education. I've recently decided to take a risk and decided to find out what entrepreneurship is about. I am enrolled in a kickass start-up training program called the Startup Institute and I am seeing it a chance to push myself in a new direction.

What's on my mind

I just got together with a good friend who's about to graduate from a masters program practice for a job interview, and realized two things: One, it takes energy and focus to step out of oneself and condense years of work and school experience into a single coherent story; it's iterative and the story can always be better. Two, it's important to be brutally honest with yourself and ask yourself what your biggest concerns are for this job, and come up with questions to ask the interviewer that get to the bottom of your concerns.

A bit about my professional background
